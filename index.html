A Look at Record Temperatures in the United States

As many folks realize, February 2017 was very warm across the United States, with record highs set across the country. In fact, the warming was so extreme that record highs topped record lows by a ratio of 50 record highs tied or broken for every record low tied or broken. Numbers like this can be hard to picture. It seems like a lot, but what does it really mean? The following visualization shows just how anomalous and extreme February 2017 was across the United States.

The map adds record highs and lows by date, while, the text loops through every record. It can take some time to make it to the end, that's part of the point of showing just how unusual the month was. If this becomes tedious, you can click the "Jump to End" button to see all records mapped.

If you'd like to explore the data more fully it's available from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.
